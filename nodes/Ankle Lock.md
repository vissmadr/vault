# Ankle Lock

ad

---

Modern ankle locks have evolved greatly.
The target of attack is different.
Instead of trying to painfully smash the achilles, we try to break the ankle.

Elbow deep grip.

Achilles grip is good because it's easy to get and control. But it sucks for breaking. Transition from achilles to half foot grip for the break.

Half foot theory.
One visual cue is to be able to see the opponent's ankle bone. If you are unable to see the ankle bone, then you have grabbed the foot too deeply.
Another visual cue is no daylight between the elbow and the opponent's leg.

No daylight.
Opponent's ankle bone visible.
Elbow on the inside of the hip.
Legs pinched together.
Hands locked together.

Two problems at all times:
- Can he kick through?
- Can he limp out?

No matter the grip.
The elbow has to be close to the center line.
Grips that allow you to have your elbow close to the center are better than the ones that don't.

Failure comes from daylight.
Failure comes from elbow away from center.
Failure come from opponent foot too deep.
Failure comes from opponent foot slip.
