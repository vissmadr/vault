# Nash Equilibrium

Game state where no player can improve their outcome by changing only their own strategy.

---

Each player is choosing the best response to the other
players' current strategies.

A Nash equilibrium does not mean the outcome is fair,
optimal, or collectively best. It only means no single player
has an incentive to unilaterally deviate.

Common in game theory, economics, negotiation, voting
systems, and competitive strategy.
